It's a Lifestyle.


Wind power or wind energy is mostly the use of wind turbines to generate electricity. Wind power is a popular, sustainable, renewable energy source that has a much smaller impact on the environment than burning fossil fuels. Historically, wind power has been used in sails, windmills and windpumps but today it is mostly used to generate electricity. Wind farms consist of many individual wind turbines, which are connected to the electric power transmission network. New onshore (on-land) wind farms are cheaper than new coal or gas plants, but expansion of wind power is being hindered by fossil fuel subsidies.Onshore wind farms have a greater visual impact on the landscape than some other power stations. Small onshore wind farms can feed some energy into the grid or provide power to isolated off-grid locations. Offshore wind farms provide a steadier and stronger source of energy and have less visual impact. Although there is less offshore wind power at present and construction and maintenance costs are higher, it is expanding.









What is Sustainability?




Sustainability is defined as: “the integration of environmental health, social equity and economic vitality in order to create thriving, healthy, diverse and resilient communities for this generation and generations to come. The practice of sustainability recognizes how these issues are interconnected and requires a systems approach and an acknowledgement of complexity.”

Sustainable practices support ecological, human, and economic health and vitality. Sustainability presumes that resources are finite, and should be used conservatively and wisely with a view to long-term priorities and consequences of the ways in which resources are used. In simplest terms, sustainability is about our children and our grandchildren, and the world we will leave them.









Sustainability is a societal goal that broadly aims for humans to safely co-exist on planet Earth over a long time. Specific definitions of sustainability are difficult to agree on and therefore vary in the literature and over time. Sustainability is commonly described along the lines of three dimensions (also called pillars): environmental, economic and social. This concept can be used to guide decisions at the global, national and at the individual level (e.g. sustainable living). In everyday usage, sustainability is often focused mostly on the environmental aspects so that "sustainability" becomes the same as "environmental sustainability". Consideration of human impacts on the environment and work to reduce negative impacts are linked to improving environmental sustainability. The most dominant environmental issues since about the year 2000 have been climate change, loss of biodiversity, loss of ecosystem services, land degradation, and air and water pollution. Humanity is now exceeding several "planetary boundaries" at the global level.

Transportation

A major source of CO2 is transport and not just a private jet but all kinds of transport from the car you drive to work everyday to a local bus or a lorry.

A way we can reduce this is by fleet management. This allows real time view on routes, fuel consumption and vehicle acquisition and disposal. Being able to track, everything allows you to assess and finds ways to become more sustainable e.g., reduce fuel consumption and costs. Additionally, it is simpler to handle costs such as maintenance, driver safety and insurance. Due to always knowing the location, driver behaviour and vehicle maintenance.

This software can also be implemented for local buses since it will allow the buses to take more efficient routes, create a better work environment for drivers and better experience for customers. A better environment allows more people to take the bus. This will not only decrease CO2 emissions significantly but develop success in the long run.

Technology

Sustainable technology can be used in all stages of becoming sustainable. From starting to substitute non-renewable to renewable resources. Then preventing negative environmental impacts in the use or production. Lastly, it is efficient and helps you maintain your efficiency in use of energy and resources.

An example of a technology is Blockchain. Blockchain is a sustainable application which improves access to shared, trusted data. It modifies the mechanism from proof of work (energy intensives) to a proof of stake mechanism (energy efficient). This is done through:
  1. Supply chain management
  2. Improved auditing
  3. Carbon emissions tracking
  4. Materials sourcing
